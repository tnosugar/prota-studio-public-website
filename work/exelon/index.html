Challenge

Exelon's Exelorate Operating System, the corporate innovation arm, was incubating EcoCred, a consumer-facing carbon-footprint app intended to become a standalone digital business. The team had a built MVP, an Exelon-Growth-Board governance gate, and formal Series-A readiness criteria to clear. The validation question was operational: can a regulated-utility-incubated consumer app run a continuous-experiment cadence that compounds into Series-A-grade evidence?

Solution

The work spanned 22 thematic sub-areas of execution and the canonical Prota validation framework, including:

  • Acquisition at scale. Paid digital across Google, Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, Pinterest, plus 16+ Instagram influencer partnerships and dedicated email and blog campaigns.
  • Continuous experimentation. A repeatable hypothesis → experiment → KPI framework, run weekly, with executive-readable monthly mid-month updates from July through November 2020.
  • Content infrastructure. Definitive content-strategy guidelines (the Sherpa archetype), 12 months of social-media editorial calendars, push-messaging editorial calendars, COVID-19 and Earth Day campaign frameworks.
  • Monetization model evolution. Affiliate and in-app advertising, then carbon-offset purchase funnels, then a standalone Shopify carbon-offset eCommerce site, then an SMB-focused B2B carbon-offset offering.
  • Operational systems. A 49-section AirTable manual codifying experiment tracking, KPI dashboards, channel libraries, and a 14-vendor services stack.
  • Series-A readiness. A Q1 2020 framework checklist scoping milestones, business model, financial forecasts, parent-fit narrative, talent plan, validation evidence, and external-investor preparation.
  • Strategic positioning research. Competitive analysis, Microsoft carbon-fee program white papers as B2B reference architecture, an Exelon-internal carbon-pilot proposal, and a Series-A target VC list.

Results

  • 1M users at the platform's peak.
  • 61,000 installs at the November 2020 measurement point.
  • CAC compressed from $3.70 in July to approximately $1.30 by October 2020, measurable acquisition efficiency gains across paid spend.
  • Sherpa-archetype content-strategy guidelines scaled across channels as the brand voice deliverable.
  • Series-A diligence checklist completed; the validation work shaped the strategic case for the parent.
  • Strategic pivot to B2B carbon offsets, the validation work clarified that the consumer voluntary-offset monetization required a different go-to-market, and reframed EcoCred toward SMB and B2B distribution.
  • Spun out and sold. Exelon spun the app out and sold it to an outside firm.

Strategic pivot

B2B-pivot signaling

The retention and monetization data made the case for reframing EcoCred from "consumer habit app" to "B2B carbon-offset platform with a B2C top-of-funnel", the canonical pattern for F500-incubated consumer products where validation reveals the app's primary value to the parent is brand and lead-gen.

What this engagement says about regulated-industry innovation.

The retention and monetization data forces a pivot toward the parent's existing strength (B2B distribution), and validation reveals that the consumer app's primary value to the parent may be brand and lead-gen rather than direct revenue.

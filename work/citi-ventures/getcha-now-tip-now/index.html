Tipping for the cashless economy, validated then pivoted.

A Citi D10X concept for stranger-to-stranger tipping (think buskers, valets, baristas) when neither party carries cash. Phase 1 validated four core assumptions on the QR badge; Phase 2 pivoted to enterprise go-to-market.

Project at a glance.

ProjectGetcha Now / Tip Now
TimelineMay 2018 to March 2019
PhasesPhase 1 (concept validation) → Phase 2 (enterprise pivot) → rebrand
OutcomePhase 1 validated four core assumptions; Phase 2 pivoted to enterprise GTM

Challenge.

The concept tested whether strangers would transact through their phones in a low-friction tipping moment. Five candidate mechanics on the table: tap-to-tap NFC, AirDrop-to-picture, QR badge, business card, app-only. The decision was which mechanic to build, against what audience, with what value proposition.

Approach.

  • Two-axis concept rejection grid: social acceptability and tech feasibility. Used as a kill-screen before user-research budget got spent.
  • Phase 1 user interviews on five mechanics. NFC failed both axes; AirDrop passed social, failed tech for cross-platform reasons; QR badge passed both.
  • Phase 2 pivoted to enterprise GTM when the consumer-side validation surfaced that the most reliable demand was for venue-mediated tipping, not stranger-to-stranger.
  • Q1 2019 rebrand closed the engagement; downstream outcome held inside Citi.

Outcome.

  • Four core assumptions validated in Phase 1 around audience, mechanic, and value proposition.
  • Two mechanics killed cleanly on the rejection grid before user-research budget went into them.
  • Pivot to enterprise GTM based on Phase 2 demand-pattern reads.

Methodology lessons.

  • Two-axis concept rejection (social acceptability and tech feasibility) for any payments product where strangers transact through their phones.
  • Tech-feasibility before user-research for hardware-dependent concepts. Run a one-page feasibility memo from the client engineering team before user-research budget gets spent.

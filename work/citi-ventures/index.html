Challenge.

Citi Ventures' D10X program runs the bank's intrapreneur-led venture pipeline. Citi employees pitch new products and advance through Seed-1, Seed-2, and Seed-3 Growth Boards before any build commits. The program needed a validation partner who could give every concept the same treatment: comparable evidence, comparable cadence, comparable kill-or-commit standard, sustained across a portfolio of bets and across years of executive turnover.

What we built across the partnership.

  • Three validation cadences run in parallel: pre-build demand tests on unbranded domains, qualitative concept-research rounds, and post-launch UX optimization with multi-sprint experiment registers.
  • The Q1 2018 six-team parallel batch (six concepts on a synchronized seven-week timeline targeting the same Growth Board date) became the canonical demand-test scaffold every later sub-project filled out.
  • A $1M-budget multi-work-order SOW from 2020, with separate work orders for demand tests and general validation services.
  • An institutional memory function across Citi sponsor cohorts: cross-engagement memory longer than any single internal sponsor's tenure.

Headline outcomes.

  • Wealth Attacker sub-segmentation became Citi's September 2020 mid-tier wealth strategy.
  • Lending persona reframe (Transactor → Often Revolver) reshaped the FlexPay-to-SpendFriend product hypothesis.
  • Sum-It MVP validated, built, shipped to Google Play; design effort cut 50%, tech 15%.
  • Workplace Banking pivoted from a BAU offer to "Banking for Working Families" on the demand-test signal.

What this engagement says about validation at scale.

Multi-year MSAs survive executive turnover, and the consultant's job is to be the institutional memory. The Wealth Attacker sub-segmentation, the Lending persona reframe, the Workplace Banking family pivot, and the recurring family-banking thesis all required someone holding cross-engagement memory longer than any single internal sponsor's tenure. That role is the consultant's defensible value in long-MSA enterprise relationships.

Case study

Citi Ventures — Nine years, 100-plus experiments.

A continuous-validation partnership inside Citi Ventures' D10X intrapreneur-led venture portfolio. Twenty-eight documented sub-projects spanning wealth, lending, workplace banking, family finance, ESG, conversational AI, and LATAM. The methodology stayed the same shape across the entire run — and produced comparable signal across bets that Citi's leadership could trust.

The challenge.

Citi's D10X program funds Citi employees to launch businesses on Growth-Board cadences — seeds go to builds go to scaling decisions — across a portfolio of concurrent bets. Each Growth Board needed defensible, comparable evidence to make a kill-or-commit call. The internal product teams could ship; what they couldn't do was run validation at startup speed inside enterprise governance, brand, security, and political constraints.

The partnership challenge was structural, not tactical. Different intrapreneurs, different concepts, different audiences — but the same Growth Board cadence, the same evidence standard, the same comparability requirement. The only methodology that survives that scope is one that produces directly comparable signal across every bet.

The solution — three cadences, one signature.

Pre-build

Demand tests

Unbranded landing page, paid traffic, structured form capture. The bulk of the work — most D10X intrapreneur concepts started here.

Concept-shape

Qualitative rounds

Screener-driven 10-12 telephone or Zoom interviews with brand-permission tests. Used when the concept needs language and frame, not numbers.

Post-launch

UX optimization

Heuristic audit + iterative design rounds against legal friction. Used when a built product needs to convert better at the same scale.

The Q1 2018 six-team parallel-batch — six concepts on a synchronized seven-week timeline targeting one Growth Board, with task-cell-level Citi/Bionic/Prota ownership — became the canonical operating signature.

Results.

Strategic — segmentation that Citi adopted

Wealth Attacker sub-segmentation work (Corporate Executives, Doctors-Dentists, Entrepreneurs, Pro Athletes) was canonized in Citi's September 2020 Mid-Tier Segmentation Approach — direct downstream of the engagement. Lending repositioned its existing FlexPay product from Transactors to Often-Revolvers; SpendFriend pulled 3.5× industry CTR at $10.02 CPL.

Operational — the rare validation-to-build-to-shipped-to-sunset arc

Sum-It MVP was D10X's clearest worked example. Two demand tests, one concept test, an unbranded Android MVP on Google Play with 17 in-flight experiments across 8 sprints. 134 real users at $209.21 CAC. Engagement sunset autumn 2019 with a clean 60-day wind-down — but reduced the eventual Citi-branded build's design effort by 50% and tech effort by 15%.

Methodological — patterns that became portable

Across nine years, the partnership produced patterns Prota now uses across every engagement: demand test as needs discovery (paid traffic to an unbranded LP produces a behavioral need-map no survey produces); life-event targeting outperforms demographic / career-stage segmentation (validated explicitly in Wealth Attacker Sprint 5 — switching from Family Liquidity to profession-based audiences degraded CPL 4× at p<0.01); comparative parallel tracks (BAU + D10X) produce politically inarguable findings when the reframing wins.

Durability — multi-year MSA survives executive turnover

Across nine years, Citi went through multiple changes in D10X strategy, ownership, and personnel. The partnership survived each one. The codified methodology was the durable artifact — not a single relationship.

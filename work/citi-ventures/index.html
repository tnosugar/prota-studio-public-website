Challenge

Citi Ventures' D10X program runs the bank's intrapreneur-led venture pipeline, Citi employees pitching new products, advancing through Seed-1, Seed-2, and Seed-3 Growth Boards before any build commits. The program needed a validation partner who could give every concept the same treatment: comparable evidence, comparable cadence, comparable kill-or-commit standard.

Solution

  • 28 distinct sub-projects across the partnership, wealth, lending, family banking, earned-wage access, ESG investing, conversational AI, identity and payments, post-launch UX optimization, multi-market LATAM tests.
  • Three validation cadences run in parallel: pre-build demand tests on unbranded domains; qualitative concept-research rounds; post-launch UX optimization with eight-sprint experiment registers.
  • Q1 2018 six-team parallel batch, six concepts on a synchronized seven-week timeline targeting the same Growth Board date. The canonical demand-test scaffold every later sub-project filled out.
  • A $1M-budget multi-work-order SOW from 2020, separate work orders for demand tests and general validation services, with a four-tier retainer rate card and per-recruit / per-interview qualitative costs codified.

Results

  • 9-year partnership sustained across multiple sponsor cohorts and program-shape evolutions.
  • 100-plus experiments producing comparable kill-or-commit calls inside D10X.
  • Wealth Attacker sub-segmentation became Citi's September 2020 Mid-Tier Segmentation Approach.
  • Lending persona reframe (Transactor to Often Revolver) reshaped the FlexPay-to-SpendFriend product hypothesis on the strength of the demand-test data.
  • Sum-It MVP, the rare validation-to-build case: validated, built, shipped to Google Play, ran live for three months acquiring real users; reduced eventual Citi-branded design effort by roughly 50% and tech effort by roughly 15%.
  • Workplace Banking pivoted from a BAU offer to "Banking for Working Families" on the strength of unprompted demand-test signal.

Selected experiments.

Wealth strategy

Wealth Attacker, Mid-tier wealth strategy

Five sequential narrowing sprints (April-October 2020). Switched from socio-economic to life-events targeting in Sprint 2 (Divorce, Family Liquidity, Business Liquidity). Sub-segmentation landed on four target segments, Corporate Executives, Doctors and Dentists, Entrepreneurs, Professional Athletes, and became Citi's mid-tier wealth strategy.

Persona reframe

Lending, FlexPay to SpendFriend

Persona reframe driven by the demand-test data. Original product hypothesis (Transactors discounting to revolve a single purchase) tested weak. Often Revolvers tested strong. Balance Bifurcation surfaced as the precondition design change. The reshape went into Citi's lending roadmap.

Validation to build

Sum-It MVP, Validation to build

Two demand tests greenlit a build. We built and shipped a publicly-distributed unbranded Android app under sum-it.co in April 2019. Three months in market, 134 real users acquired at blended $209 CAC across $28K of spend. Closed cleanly. Reduced design effort for the eventual Citi-branded version by roughly 50%, tech effort by roughly 15%.

Comparative tracks

Workplace Banking, BAU vs D10X

Comparative validation: BAU offer and D10X reframe ran on the same audience and channel mix. The reframe outperformed; the political case for change was inarguable. Sprint 2 pivoted entirely to "Banking for Working Families."

ESG investing

Workplace Banking, Impact Banking track

Two-year-later rediscovery of the values-led investing thesis surfaced earlier in Impact-Investful (2018). D2C digital bank with ESG-aligned deposits, ethical-footprint tracker, recycled-plastic card, tested under a fresh unbranded domain. Same strategic thesis, sharper surface.

Post-launch UX

Out Of The Red, Post-launch UX optimization

Heuristic content-and-flow audit on a shipped Citi product. Onboarding density, alerts, account linkage, and end-to-end-flow integration ran as a forward backlog of next experiments. The cleanest example in the archive of post-launch retainer-style validation work.

What this engagement says about validation at scale.

Multi-year MSAs survive executive turnover, and the consultant's job is to be the institutional memory. The Wealth Attacker sub-segmentation, the Lending Often Revolver reframe, the Workplace Banking family pivot, and the recurring family-banking thesis all required someone holding the cross-engagement memory longer than any single internal sponsor's tenure. That role is the consultant's defensible value in long-MSA enterprise relationships.

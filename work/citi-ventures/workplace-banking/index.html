When BAU lost the demand test.

A Citi Ventures D10X engagement that ran two product hypotheses head-to-head on the same audience and channel mix. The D10X reframe outperformed the BAU offer. The political case for change was inarguable.

Challenge.

Citi had an existing employer-channel banking benefits offer (BAU) targeting workplaces and their employees. D10X had a different read on the audience: that family and parenting overlays would outperform career-maturity framing for the same employee pool. The decision Citi brought us was which framing to take forward. The political dynamic added complexity. The BAU stakeholder team had built the existing offer and held a roadmap commitment to it. The D10X team had a reframe hypothesis and wanted to test it cleanly.

Approach.

  • Comparative parallel-track design. The BAU offer and the D10X reframe ran on the same audience and channel mix at the same time, so the comparative read was uncontaminated by audience or channel differences.
  • Family and parenting overlay tested explicitly as the D10X reframe variable.
  • ESG track ran in parallel as a third hypothesis, picking up the values-led-investing thesis Citi had surfaced two years earlier in Impact-Investful (2018).
  • Sprint 2 pivoted entirely to "Banking for Working Families" when the Sprint 1 read came in clean.

Outcome.

  • D10X reframe outperformed the BAU offer on the same audience and channel mix.
  • Pivoted to "Banking for Working Families" in Sprint 2 on the strength of the comparative signal.
  • ESG track validated as a parallel direction, picked up later as Impact Banking.
  • Political case for the reframe became inarguable, the comparative test design was the structural reason the political stakes resolved.

Methodology lessons.

  • Comparative parallel tracks (BAU plus D10X) produce politically inarguable findings. When Prota's reframing outperforms the BAU offer on the same audience and channel, the political case is structural, not subjective.
  • Family and parenting overlays outperform career-maturity for employer-channel banking benefits.
  • Use parallel tracks deliberately for engagements with politically committed roadmaps. The friction with the BAU team is real but bounded, and the comparative output justifies it.

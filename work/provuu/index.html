Hardware Kickstarter · Outdoor tech · Startup

The highest-funded ski project in Kickstarter history.

Four-phase Kickstarter program for Provuu, a Swiss outdoor-tech startup building XR snow goggles. Fully funded within the first hour. Five times over by Christmas. ISPO Brandnew Award won mid-campaign. The canonical Prota reference for hardware-launch acquisition with a fixed external deadline.

Challenge

By September 2025 Provuu had a near-finished hardware prototype, an ISPO Brandnew Award nomination, and a hard need: convert the engineering into commercial signal at Kickstarter pre-order volume to fund the production run. The validation question took the form "will the market pre-order 2,000 units at CHF 550 within 60 days?", with a $1M revenue goal and a fixed external calendar (Slush Helsinki, Black Friday, ISPO Munich, Kickstarter live through January 1, 2026).

Solution

A four-phase paid-and-owned acquisition program executed against tight external dates.

  • Phase 1, Planning (4-5 weeks). Discovery, strategy, accounts, creative, analytics, social, landing pages.
  • Phase 2, Test (3-4 weeks). Limited-budget channel testing and account warm-up across Meta and Google.
  • Phase 3, Lead Gen (3-4 weeks). Pre-launch acquisition driving waitlist and pre-orders.
  • Phase 4, Campaign (4-5 weeks). Kickstarter launch in front of Black Friday and ISPO Munich, running through January 1, 2026.

The program orchestrated:

  • Multi-channel paid media across Meta (two campaigns: Core Countries and Rest of Europe) and Google (Search non-brand, Display, PMax), fourteen-country audience, premium-brand and avalanche-safety interest stacks.
  • Phased landing-page architecture on Squarespace, pre-launch waitlist hero, interim award-update variant carrying the ISPO Brandnew banner, then a Kickstarter-live home page swapping CTA to Buy on Kickstarter. A single argument across versions; CTA rotated by phase.
  • Eleven-stage email drip across pre-launch, in-campaign, and final-week.
  • Weekly content calendar themed around Product Feature, Product Milestone, Event (Sölden World Cup, ISPO), and Snow Report per resort.
  • Viral-Loops referral mechanic with Mailchimp integration, leaderboard reset on Black Friday, top-referrer prizes plus random-draw winners, each fulfilled through per-winner Kickstarter secret-reward links.
  • Earned-media collaboration, long-form Freeskier magazine feature plus follow-up email blast post-funding-milestone.
  • Physical-channel ad system, CityLight 112x168 outdoor formats, ISPO booth banner / leaflet / logo panel, Freeskier print, each anchored on a single one-word disruption (SNOW? / CLOUDS? / WHITEOUT?) followed by the consistent payoff.

Results

  • Highest-funded ski project in Kickstarter history. Surpassed Carv mid-campaign.
  • Fully funded within the first hour of the Kickstarter going live.
  • Five times over its initial funding goal by Christmas.
  • $189,853 from 320 backers at the 27-days-to-go reporting milestone.
  • ISPO Brandnew Award won mid-campaign in the Outdoor / Winter Sports category.
  • Single-line value-prop architecture survived intact across landing pages, paid social, search, email, and physical-channel ad formats.

Selected experiments.

Program scaffold

Defensible four-phase Kickstarter scaffold

Planning / Test / Lead Gen / Campaign, sequenced against fixed external dates (Black Friday, ISPO Munich, Kickstarter live window). Each phase had its own budget envelope, success criteria, and exit gate. Reusable for any hardware-launch program with a Kickstarter or pre-order mechanic.

Conversion architecture

Layered conversion mechanic

Every prospect had a calibrated next step: ad → waitlist landing page → email drip → ISPO Brandnew announcement → Black Friday early-access → Kickstarter pre-order → referral leaderboard → final-week purchase. No prospect was ever a step away from a viable conversion.

Value prop

Single-line value-prop architecture

Odds of injury 3x higher in poor visibility → VIEW-XR boosts contrast 75% / range 5x → Swiss-developed, athlete-validated → tech specs → FAQ → CTA. The same argument held across long-form landing page, twelve Meta primary-text variants, two Google PMax sheets, six personal-story testimonial briefs, and a six-format physical-channel ad system.

Audience expansion

Six personal-story testimonial briefs

Targeted at flat-light and low-vision pain, older skiers, retirees, eye-condition advocates, ski instructors with prior accident history. Framed accessibility as a wedge, not just performance. Built breadth of audience the performance-first creative could not reach alone.

Referral velocity

Viral-Loops referral leaderboard

1 point per referred signup, leaderboard reset on Black Friday, three top-referrer prizes plus two random-draw prizes, each winner fulfilled through a per-winner Kickstarter secret-reward link. Drove waitlist quality and sharing velocity into the Kickstarter window.

Earned media

Freeskier collaboration

A single long-form story authored as a co-production with Freeskier magazine, then re-amplified through a Freeskier follow-up email blast post-funding-milestone. The kind of earned-media moment that paid acquisition cannot manufacture.

What this engagement says about hardware Kickstarter validation.
